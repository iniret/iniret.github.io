10 Makeup Secrets From Ancient Egypt That Still Work Today

The ancient Egyptians were known for their beauty and makeup secrets, and many of these age-old techniques are still relevant today. From the use of kohl to create a dramatic eye look to the use of henna to dye hair, the Egyptians had a variety of beauty secrets that are still used today. Here are 10 makeup secrets from ancient Egypt that still work today.

1. Kohl

Kohl was a popular makeup product used by the ancient Egyptians to create a dramatic eye look. It was made from a combination of lead, antimony, and other minerals, and was applied to the eyes to create a smoky, sultry look. Today, kohl is still used to create a dramatic eye look, and is available in a variety of colors.

2. Henna

Henna was used by the ancient Egyptians to dye their hair and nails. It was made from a plant-based dye and was used to create a variety of colors, from red to black. Today, henna is still used to dye hair and nails, and is available in a variety of colors.

3. Red Ochre

Red ochre was a popular makeup product used by the ancient Egyptians to create a natural blush. It was made from a combination of iron oxide and clay, and was applied to the cheeks to create a rosy glow. Today, red ochre is still used to create a natural blush, and is available in a variety of shades.

4. Beeswax

Beeswax was used by the ancient Egyptians to create a glossy lip look. It was made from a combination of beeswax and other natural oils, and was applied to the lips to create a glossy, shiny look. Today, beeswax is still used to create a glossy lip look, and is available in a variety of colors.

5. Castor Oil

Castor oil was used by the ancient Egyptians to create a natural eyeliner. It was made from a combination of castor oil and other natural oils, and was applied to the eyes to create a dramatic, smoky look. Today, castor oil is still used to create a natural eyeliner, and is available in a variety of colors.

6. Alabaster

Alabaster was used by the ancient Egyptians to create a natural highlight. It was made from a combination of alabaster and other natural oils, and was applied to the face to create a glowing, radiant look. Today, alabaster is still used to create a natural highlight, and is available in a variety of shades.

7. Frankincense

Frankincense was used by the ancient Egyptians to create a natural bronzer. It was made from a combination of frankincense and other natural oils, and was applied to the face to create a sun-kissed look. Today, frankincense is still used to create a natural bronzer, and is available in a variety of shades.

8. Myrrh

Myrrh was used by the ancient Egyptians to create a natural contour. It was made from a combination of myrrh and other natural oils, and was applied to the face to create a sculpted, chiseled look. Today, myrrh is still used to create a natural contour, and is available in a variety of shades.

9. Galena

Galena was used by the ancient Egyptians to create a natural eyeshadow. It was made from a combination of galena and other natural oils, and was applied to the eyes to create a smoky, sultry look. Today, galena is still used to create a natural eyeshadow, and is available in a variety of colors.

10. Mastic

Mastic was used by the ancient Egyptians to create a natural lip color. It was made from a combination of mastic and other natural oils, and was applied to the lips to create a natural, rosy look. Today, mastic is still used to create a natural lip color, and is available in a variety of shades.

From the use of kohl to create a dramatic eye look to the use of henna to dye hair, the Egyptians had a variety of beauty secrets that are still used today. Whether you're looking to create a dramatic eye look or a natural lip color, these 10 makeup secrets from ancient Egypt are sure to help you achieve the look you desire.
