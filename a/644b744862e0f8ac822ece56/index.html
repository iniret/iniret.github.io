Antioxidants are compounds that help protect your cells from damage caused by free radicals. Free radicals are molecules that can damage your cells and lead to premature aging, wrinkles, and other skin problems.

Eating antioxidant-rich foods can help protect your skin from free radical damage and keep it looking healthy and glowing. Here are 7 antioxidant-rich superfoods that you should add to your diet for healthy, glowing skin.

1. Blueberries

Blueberries are one of the most antioxidant-rich foods you can eat. They are packed with vitamins A, C, and E, as well as flavonoids and anthocyanins. These compounds help protect your skin from free radical damage and keep it looking healthy and glowing.

2. Dark Chocolate

Dark chocolate is a great source of antioxidants. It contains flavonoids, which help protect your skin from free radical damage and keep it looking healthy and glowing. Plus, dark chocolate is delicious and can be enjoyed as a healthy snack.

3. Green Tea

Green tea is packed with antioxidants, including catechins and polyphenols. These compounds help protect your skin from free radical damage and keep it looking healthy and glowing. Plus, green tea is a great way to get your daily dose of antioxidants.

4. Avocados

Avocados are a great source of antioxidants, including vitamins A, C, and E. These compounds help protect your skin from free radical damage and keep it looking healthy and glowing. Plus, avocados are a delicious and nutritious addition to any meal.

5. Tomatoes

Tomatoes are a great source of antioxidants, including lycopene. Lycopene helps protect your skin from free radical damage and keep it looking healthy and glowing. Plus, tomatoes are a delicious and nutritious addition to any meal.

6. Walnuts

Walnuts are a great source of antioxidants, including vitamin E. Vitamin E helps protect your skin from free radical damage and keep it looking healthy and glowing. Plus, walnuts are a delicious and nutritious snack.

7. Spinach

Spinach is a great source of antioxidants, including vitamins A, C, and E. These compounds help protect your skin from free radical damage and keep it looking healthy and glowing. Plus, spinach is a delicious and nutritious addition to any meal.
